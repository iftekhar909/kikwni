That means:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>You can’t just toss it in the washer without reading the label</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Some require hand-washing or spot cleaning</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>You may need a commercial washer for heavy blankets</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Taking shortcuts can lead to ripped seams, clumped fillings, and bad odors that won’t go away.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 1: Check the Label First</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Before doing anything, <strong>read the care label</strong>. But when it comes to cleaning it, things can get a bit tricky. Unlike regular blankets, heated blankets contain wiring and electrical components that require careful handling.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>In this detailed guide, you’ll learn how to safely wash a heated blanket, including prep steps, machine vs. hand washing methods, drying instructions, and maintenance tips to extend its life without compromising safety.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Why Cleaning Your Heated Blanket Matters</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Over time, heated blankets collect:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Sweat</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Skin cells</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Dust and allergens</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Body oils</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If not cleaned regularly, your cozy companion can start to smell, trigger allergies, or wear out faster. But since it contains electrical wiring, you need to follow the right process to avoid damage.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 1: Check the Manufacturer’s Label</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Before you do anything, <strong>read the care tag or user manual</strong>. It will tell you:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>If the blanket is machine washable</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>What water temperature to use</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Whether tumble drying is safe</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Special precautions to take</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p><strong>Warning:</strong> Never assume a heated blanket is safe to machine wash unless the label specifically says so.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 2: Unplug and Detach Controls</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>All electric blankets have a power cord and control module. Before washing:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Unplug the blanket from the outlet</strong></li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Remove the control unit</strong> (if detachable)</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Check for any exposed wiring or damage</strong></li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>You should <strong>never submerge any part that connects to electricity</strong>. If your blanket has non-detachable components, it’s likely spot-clean only.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 3: Inspect for Wear and Tear</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Look for signs of:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Frayed cords</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Burn marks or melted spots</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Exposed wires</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Cracks near the plug</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If you spot any of these, do <strong>not</strong> wash the blanket. In fact, stop using it and replace it. Washing a damaged electric blanket can be dangerous.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 4: Spot Clean When Possible</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If your heated blanket only has a small area that needs attention, spot cleaning is best:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Mix a solution of mild detergent and cold water</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Dip a soft cloth or sponge into the solution</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Gently blot the stained area</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Rinse the spot with a clean, damp cloth</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Let air dry completely before reusing</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Avoid soaking any area or letting moisture seep near connection points.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 5: Machine Washing a Heated Blanket (If Allowed)</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If your blanket is labeled machine washable, follow these steps:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Use a Large Front-Loading Washer</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Avoid top loaders with agitators that can tug on wires</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Select Gentle Settings</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Cold or lukewarm water</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Delicate or gentle cycle</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Mild detergent (no bleach or fabric softener)</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Wash Alone</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Don’t mix with clothes or towels</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>The weight can damage internal wiring</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Minimize Wash Time</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Some manufacturers recommend washing for just a few minutes, then moving to rinse</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Optional: Extra Rinse</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Ensures all detergent is removed</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 6: Hand Washing a Heated Blanket (Safer for Older Models)</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If the manual discourages machine washing, or if you have an older or delicate heated blanket, hand washing may be safer.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Fill a Tub or Basin with Cold Water</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Add a small amount of mild detergent</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Submerge the Blanket (Wires Detached)</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Gently swish the blanket</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Do not twist, wring, or pull</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Soak for 10-15 Minutes</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Agitate gently by hand</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Drain and Rinse Thoroughly</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Refill tub with clean water and rinse</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Repeat if needed</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Press (Don’t Wring) to Remove Water</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Use towels to absorb excess moisture</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 7: Drying Your Heated Blanket</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Drying incorrectly is one of the biggest risks. Follow these steps carefully:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Skip the Dryer (Unless Label Permits)</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>High heat can damage wires and insulation</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>If allowed, use low heat or air dry setting only</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Air Dry Flat</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Lay the blanket flat on a clean surface</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Flip occasionally to ensure even drying</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Keep away from direct sunlight and heat sources</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Avoid Hanging</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Hanging a wet heated blanket can stretch the wiring</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Do Not Iron or Dry Clean</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Heat and chemicals from dry cleaning are dangerous</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 8: Post-Wash Safety Check</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Before plugging your blanket back in:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Inspect for any new damage</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Then:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Plug in and turn on briefly to test</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Monitor for any odd smells, sounds, or hot spots</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>If anything seems off, <strong>stop use immediately</strong>.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:separator --> <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/> <!-- /wp:separator -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --> <h3 class="wp-block-heading">How Often Should You Wash a Heated Blanket?</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>General guidelines:</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Light use:</strong> Once every 3–4 months</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Nightly use:</strong> Every 1–2 months</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li><strong>Spot clean regularly</strong> between full washes</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- /wp:list -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>The more you wash, the more strain you put on the wiring. <h3 class="wp-block-heading">Quick Answer: When Can a Baby Sleep with a Blanket?</h3> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p><strong>After 12 months old</strong> — and even then, it depends.</p> <!-- /wp:paragraph -->  <!-- wp:paragraph --> <p>Most pediatricians agree that it’s generally safe to introduce a lightweight blanket <strong>after your baby turns one</strong>, once they <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Reduced SIDS risk</li> <!-- /wp:list-item -->  <!-- wp:list-item --> <li>Some ability to push away or reposition the blanket if needed</li> <!-- /wp:list-item --></ul> <!-- <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:heading {"level":4} --> <h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Newborn to 12 Months: No Blanket</strong></h4> <!-- /wp:heading -->  <!-- wp:list --> <ul
